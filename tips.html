Useful Cooking Tips

Keep Your Knives Sharp

A sharp knife is safer and more effective than a dull one. It lets you cut ingredients more precisely and quickly.

Use Fresh Herbs

Fresh herbs like mint, parsley, and coriander enhance flavour and add colour to your meals. Add them at the end of cooking for the best taste.

Preheat Your Pan

Start with a hot pan before adding oil or ingredients. This prevents food from sticking and helps it cook evenly.

Season as You Go

Don't wait until the end to season. Add small amounts of salt, pepper, and spices during cooking so flavours build gradually.

Let Meat Rest

After cooking meat, let it rest for 5–10 minutes. This allows juices to redistribute, making it tender and juicy.

Clean As You Cook

Keep your workspace clean by washing dishes and wiping counters as you go. It saves time and reduces stress later.

Use the Right Oil

For high-heat cooking, use oils like sunflower or canola. For dressings or drizzling, use olive oil for better flavour.

Taste Frequently

Tasting while cooking helps you adjust seasoning and know when food is done. Don't be afraid to experiment!

Read the Recipe First

Always read the entire recipe before you start cooking. It helps avoid surprises and missing ingredients.

Don't Crowd the Pan

Give your food space to cook. Crowding traps steam and causes uneven browning or soggy textures.
